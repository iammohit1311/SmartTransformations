Smart City Advisory

Review and validate RFP responses. Assessment of

  • Pricing – competitiveness and the overall economy. Comparison with best practice prices.
  • Scope – key deliverables to be covered and percentage compliance.
  • Level of service commitments. Service level agreements to be on par with the best in the industry.
  • Milestones, measurement KPI of success.

Review and validate RFP design. Assessment of

  • The validity of the scope and overall objective and vision.
  • Fitment with an overall plan and integration with other projects.
  • Any possible gaps in the scope of an individual RFP and in integration with other RFPs
  • Identification of key services and service levels
  • KPI for project success

Develop a roadmap

  • Identify the set of the transformative initiatives in two or three phases, incorporating
    • Citizen’s largest concerns, the overall vision of the Municipal leadership
    • Cost and affordability
    • Progression to be backward compatible. Ensure no rework
  • Prioritize within each phase based on
    • Impact of the largest number of lives. People-centric
    • Timeframe – prioritize high impact projects with fast turnaround time
    • Cost – prioritize based on low cost and high visibility

Technology advisory-I

  • Selecting the right technology that has the largest impact at the lowest cost
  • Assessment of the integration of technology areas – from electronics to civil engineering to data management. Integration of disparate technologies for a directed result.

Technology advisory-II

  • Provide guidance on leading technologies for Smart cities – IoT devices, sensors, low power radio connectivity, broadband connectivity, energy efficiency, automation
  • Assessment of technologies for computing and data management, and command and control centre. Applications of these technologies. Key city functions to be integrated. Their service levels.
  • Sizing of the data centres and the control and command centres.

Develop financial models

  • Assessment of opportunities for funding the projects
  • Work with rating agencies in getting a Credit rating of the Municipalities.
  • External funds engagements
  • Valuation of projects

Develop partnership models and contracts

  • Develop the contractual framework.
  • Develop executable service agreements along with measurable service levels.
  • Fair and executable terms and conditions, yet ensuring performance and compliance.

Project governance, program governance, reporting

  • Develop a governance framework for each project, their integration with each other and integration with the larger roadmap.
  • Develop an integrated program management structure.
  • Identify key stakeholders at various levels for each project and programs, and ensure their engagement and inputs.

Reporting

  • Develop report structure for each project, program and the roadmap. These reports to reflect the true status of progress.
  • Report structure for reporting to the public and to the governing councils.
